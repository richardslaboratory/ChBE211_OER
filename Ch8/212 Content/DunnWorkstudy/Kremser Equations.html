Forms of Kremser Equation:#

Form A: $\( \frac{y_{N+1} - y_j^*}{y_1 - y_1^*} = \left( \frac{1 - \left( \frac{L}{mV}^{N+1} \right)}{1 - \frac{L}{mV}} \right) \)$

\[ N = \frac{\ln \left[ \left(1 - \frac{mV}{L} \right) \left(\frac{y_{N+1} - y_1^*}{y_1 - y_1^*} \right) + \frac{mV}{L} \right]}{\ln \left(\frac{L}{mV}\right)} \]

eqn 12-21 Wankat

Key points on McCabe-Thiele plot:

  • \(y_{N+1}\) is often given.

  • \(y_1\) could be calculated from operating line equation if \(x_0\), \(\frac{L}{V}\) known or could be specified

  • \(y_1^*\) could be specified where the \(x = x_0\) intersects with the equilibrium line.

  • If there were an eq. stag 0, it is the value of \(y\) that would be in eqiulibrium with \(x_0\).

  • More importantly, this value of \(y\) helps us calculate one of the \(\Delta y \) we needed for Kremser equation derivation ( \(\Delta y_0 \)).

Form B:

eqn 12-24 Wankat

\[ \frac{y_{N+1} - y_{N+1}^*}{y_1 - y_1^*} = \left( \frac{L}{mV} \right)^N \]
\[ N = \ln \frac{\left[ \left( \frac{y_{N+1} - y_{N+1}^*}{y_1 - y_1^*} \right) \right] }{ \ln \left( \frac{L}{mV} \right) }\]

Form B still uses \(y_1^*\), \( y_1\), and \(y_{N+1}\)

New: \(y_{N+1}^*\) = mx_{N} + b $

Helps calculate last \( \Delta y \) of analytical approach.
