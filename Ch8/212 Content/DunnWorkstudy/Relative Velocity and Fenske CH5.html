\[ \require{cancel} \]

Relative volatility:

\[ \alpha_{AB} = \frac{K_A}{K_B} = \frac{y_A/x_A}{y_B/x_B} \]

Relative volatility can be constant on a single stage:

\[ \frac{y_A}{y_B} = \alpha_{AB} \cdot \frac{x_A}{x_B} \]

Note notation on \(\alpha\):

\[ \alpha_{AB} = \frac{K_A}{K_B} \]
\[ \alpha_{BA} = \frac{K_B}{K_A} \]

Could be:

\[ \alpha_{12} = \frac{K_1}{K_2} \]
\[ \alpha_{21} = \frac{K_2}{K_1} \]

etc.

Fenske Equation#

  1. Total Reflux → Look at reboiler

some figure#

We would have overbars for L and V but are going to drop them here. The reboiler can be denoted as stage R or stage N+1.

Mass Bal around reboiler at total reflux (No bottom = product B = 0)

\[ V_{N+1} y_{N+1} = L_N x_N \text{(for a single component)}\]

Similar to a total condenser:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned} V_{N+1} y_{N+1} = L_N x_N \quad \text{or} \quad y_{N+1} = x_N $$ for a component A, we can write this as:\\$$ y_{A,R} = x_{A,N}, \quad y_{B,R} = x_{B,N} \end{aligned}\end{align} \]

At reboiler:

\[ \left[\frac{y_{A,R}}{y_{B,R}}\right] = \left[\frac{x_{A,N}}{x_{B,N}}\right] \]

→ Operating line is \(y = x\) line. (recall our graphical method of finding \(N_{min}\).

In general, for equilibrium:

\[ \left[\frac{y_A}{y_B}\right]_R = \alpha_R \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_R \]
\[ \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_N = \alpha_R \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_R \]

Blue underlined terms are equilibrium substitution in the right-hand side of the above equation.

some figure#

Mass balance around stage N:

\[ V_{N+1} y_{N+1} + L_{N-1} x_{N-1} = L_N x_N + V_N y_N \]
\[ V_R y_R + L_{N-1} x_{N-1} = L_N x_N + V_N y_N \]
\[ \text{From before} \quad V_R y_R = L_N x_N \]
\[ L_{N-1} - x_{N-1} = V_N y_N \]
\[ \Rightarrow x_{N-1} = y_N \]
\[ \Rightarrow x_{A,N-1} = y_{A,N}, \quad x_{B,N-1} = y_{B,N} \]

Equilibrium relationship at stage N#

\[ \left[\frac{y_A}{y_B}\right]_N = \alpha_N \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_N \]
\[ \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B}\right]_{N-1} = \alpha_N \left[ \frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_N \]
Recall
\[ \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_N = \alpha_R \left[ \frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_R \]
\[ \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_{N-1} = \alpha_N \alpha_R \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_R \]

Can keep walking up the column alternating between solving mass bal, using eq. relationship to get to the point

\[ \left[ \frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_D = \alpha_1, \alpha_2, \ldots, \alpha_{N-1},\alpha_N, \alpha_R \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_R \]

At the distillate stream

The definition of a geometric mean is:

\[ \alpha_{AB} = \left[\alpha_1 \alpha_2 \alpha_3 \ldots \alpha_{N-1} \alpha_{N} \alpha_R \right]^{1/N} \rightarrow ??? N = N_{\text{min}} \]
\[ \alpha_{AB} N_{\text{min}} = \left[\alpha_1 \ldots \alpha_R \right] \]

We will use this to characterize the geometric average relative volatility

\[ \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_D = \alpha_{AB}N_{\text{min}} \left[\frac{x_A}{x_B} \right]_R \]
\[ N_{\text{min}} = \frac{\ln \left[ \left[ \frac{x_A}{x_B}\right]_D / \left[ \frac{x_A}{x_B}\right]_R\right]}{\ln \alpha_{AB}} \]

One form of the Fenske Equation works best for constant relative volatility.

To use in terms of fractional recovery…

\[ N_{\text{min}} = \frac{ \ln \left[ \frac{\left[ \frac{Dx_A}{Dx_B} \right]_D}{\left[ \frac{Bx_A}{Bx_B} \right]_R} \right] } {\ln \alpha_{AB}}\]
\[ N_{\text{min}} = \frac{ \ln \left[ \frac{f_{?A} \cdot \cancel{F_{?A}}}{f_{?B} \cdot \cancel{F_{?B}}} \cdot \frac{f_{B_1B} \cdot \cancel{F_{2B}}}{f_{B_1A} \cdot \cancel{F_{2A}}} \right] } {\ln \alpha_{AB}}\]
\[ = \frac{\ln \left[ \frac{f_{D_1A} \cdot f_{B_1B}}{(1 - f_{B_1B})(1 - f_{D_1A})} \right]}{\ln \alpha_{AB}} \]

~~ Fenske for fractional recovery.
